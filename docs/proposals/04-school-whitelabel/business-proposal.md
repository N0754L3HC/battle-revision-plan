# Business Proposal: School White-Label Platform

**Prepared by:** Battle Plan  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Proposal ref:** BP-004

---

## Executive Summary

The same product that students use individually can be sold to schools as a whole-department tool with a teacher dashboard on top. One deal with a school's Head of Maths is worth more than 200 individual student subscriptions — and requires no marketing to students at all. Schools already buy revision tools (Tassomai, Dr Frost, Seneca), but none of them show teachers what their students are actually doing with past papers. This proposal positions Battle Plan as the first past-paper performance tracking platform designed for school-wide use: students use it exactly as today, and teachers see everything in one place.

---

## The Problem

### For teachers

Teachers assign past papers constantly, but tracking what students actually score — and why they lose marks — is entirely manual. Mark books are updated per-teacher, per-class. There is no school-wide view of which topics are weak across a year group. When a Head of Department asks "how is Year 13 doing on Organic Chemistry?", the honest answer is "I don't know."

### For schools

Schools spend £3,000–£15,000 per year on revision platforms (Seneca Learning costs £1,200/department/yr, Dr Frost ~£800/yr). These platforms focus on content delivery. None of them close the loop on past paper performance — the metric that actually predicts exam results.

### For students

Students already use free tools to track their own scores. The school buying them an account means they get cloud sync, teacher visibility, and a shared resource library — for free, from their perspective.

## The Product

### Student experience (unchanged)

Students log in exactly as they do now. Their past paper scores, errors, and exam countdown remain their own. The only visible difference: a subtle "Shared with [School Name]" tag on their score history, if they have consented.

### Teacher dashboard

A separate authenticated portal for teachers:

**Class overview:**
- All students' latest paper scores, by subject and paper
- Class average, highest, lowest — updated in real time
- Traffic light view: green (on track), amber (slipping), red (at risk)

**Topic weakness heatmap:**
- Which topics appear most in the error logs across the class
- Filterable by error type (calculation error, method error, forgot content)
- Exportable to CSV for departmental review

**Individual student view:**
- Full score history for any student in their class
- Error log with topic breakdown
- Grade trajectory: are they improving, stable, or declining?

**Exam countdown integration:**
- Shows teacher which papers are upcoming and which students have done zero practice for that subject recently

**Alerts:**
- Automated flag when a student has not logged a paper in 14+ days
- Flag when a student's score drops 10+ points between papers
- Flag when a student is logging recurring errors in the same topic

### School admin panel

Head of Sixth Form / Head of Department role:
- Add/remove teachers
- Create class groups (link students to teachers)
- Export whole-school data for progress review meetings
- Manage school branding (logo, colour on student-facing app)

## Go-to-Market

### Phase 1 — Free pilots with warm contacts (months 1–2)

Approach 5 schools with a free 1-term pilot. Target: heads of department at schools with strong A-level programmes. Offer free access in exchange for a case study and a reference. A teacher testimonial saying "I can now see which topics my Year 13 class are consistently losing marks on" is worth more than any ad spend.

### Phase 2 — Direct outreach (months 3–6)

Email campaigns to Heads of Maths and Science at 500 sixth forms. Use TES (Times Educational Supplement) teacher forum for community presence. Apply to TeachMeet events and subject association CPD days (Mathematical Association, ASE).

### Phase 3 — Academy chain deals (months 7–12)

Approach multi-academy trusts (MATs) — one deal covers 5–20 schools. Target: Harris Federation, Ark Schools, Oasis Community Learning. One MAT deal at £3,500/yr is equivalent to 7 department licences and requires 1 sales conversation.

### Phase 4 — Channel partnerships

Partner with A-level revision publishers (CGP, Oxford Revise) or exam board CPD teams who already have relationships with school departments. Revenue share on referrals.

## Competitive Advantage

- **Past paper focus.** Seneca, Tassomai, and Memrise focus on content delivery. Battle Plan tracks what matters to exam boards: past paper performance.
- **Student-led data.** Students log their own scores — teachers get honest data, not teacher-collected data that students inflate.
- **Lightweight adoption.** No new curriculum integration, no LMS import. Students download an app, teachers get a link. Zero IT procurement overhead.
- **Price.** £499/yr is below the procurement threshold at most schools — a HOD can approve it without a committee.

---

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| GDPR / student data concerns | Consent-first design; school is a data controller, Battle Plan is a processor; DPA in place |
| Slow procurement cycles | Price below £500 to avoid formal tender; target HODs not SLT |
| Teachers not using the dashboard | Embed in existing workflows: send weekly automated email summaries to teachers |
| Students not opting in to sharing | Frame it as accountability tool, not surveillance; default opt-in at school-licensed schools |

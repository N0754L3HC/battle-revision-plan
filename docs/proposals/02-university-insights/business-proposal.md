# Business Proposal: University Insights & Admissions Intelligence Platform

**Prepared by:** Battle Plan  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Proposal ref:** BP-002

---

## Executive Summary

Battle Plan has accumulated structured, high-intent behavioural data from A-level students: subject combinations, past paper scores over time, error patterns, exam board, and grade trajectories. UK universities currently have no reliable signal between UCAS predicted grades (notoriously inflated) and actual exam-day performance. This proposal outlines a B2B data and advertising product sold to university admissions teams and marketing departments — giving them precision targeting and predictive insight they cannot get anywhere else.

---

## The Problem

UK universities face two distinct and expensive problems:

**1. Admissions blind spots.** Predicted grades submitted via UCAS have a well-documented reliability problem — over 50% of students receive lower grades than predicted. Admissions teams make conditional offers based on these predictions with no real-time signal about which students are actually on track.

**2. Wasteful recruitment marketing.** Universities spend £3,000–£8,000 per enrolled student on marketing. Most of this is blunt: open day emails, generic social ads, careers fair stands. There is no way to target a student who is specifically studying Chemistry A2, achieving 78% on AQA past papers, and looking at courses requiring ABB.

Battle Plan solves both.

## The Product

### Tier 1 — Admissions Intelligence Reports (£8,000–£25,000/yr per institution)

Anonymous cohort-level reports delivered quarterly:

- Subject combination frequency and performance trends
- Which topics students are struggling with by board and subject
- Grade trajectory curves: what does an A* student's score progression look like vs a B student at this point in the year?
- Regional breakdowns
- Comparison: predicted vs tracked performance variance

These reports are sold to admissions policy teams and academic departments, not individuals. No personally identifiable information is included. Fully GDPR compliant.

### Tier 2 — Targeted Course Promotion (CPM/CPC advertising)

Universities pay to promote specific courses to students who match:

- Subject combination (e.g. Maths + Physics)
- Current performance band (e.g. tracking toward AAB)
- Exam board
- Time until first exam (urgency signal)
- Geographic region (for campus visit invitations)

Delivered as native cards within the Battle Plan app — contextually placed in the Resources or Exams view. Not banner ads. Not intrusive.

Pricing model: £15–40 CPM (comparable to LinkedIn B2B rates, justified by extreme audience specificity).

### Tier 3 — Conditional Offer Tracking (Premium, invite-only)

Universities share their conditional offer holders' student IDs (opt-in). Battle Plan sends anonymised weekly signals: "student is on track / showing decline." Admissions teams can prioritise outreach before results day rather than scrambling in August.

Requires explicit student consent and a data processing agreement with the institution.

## Go-to-Market

**Phase 1 (months 1–3):** Build the reporting layer, anonymise and aggregate existing data, prepare sample reports. Approach 3 post-92 universities with free pilot intelligence reports in exchange for a testimonial and a paid renewal commitment.

**Phase 2 (months 4–6):** Use pilot testimonials to approach Russell Group admissions marketing teams. Attend UCAS HE Choices conference and CASE (Council for Advancement) events.

**Phase 3 (months 7–12):** Launch self-serve advertising portal for smaller universities. Hire one higher education sales specialist on commission.

## Competitive Advantage

- **Data no one else has.** Not survey data, not school-reported data — actual paper-by-paper performance tracked longitudinally by the students themselves.
- **Intent signal.** A student using a revision app is actively preparing for their exams. This is the highest-intent moment in a student's academic journey.
- **Cost.** Cheaper per enrolled student than any current acquisition channel.

---

## Legal & Ethical Framework

- All individual data anonymised and aggregated before sale
- Student consent captured at account creation (analytics opt-in)
- Data processing agreements in place with all institutional customers
- ICO registration maintained
- Students can opt out of the analytics cohort at any time
- No sale of individual-level data under any circumstances

---

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Student backlash if data use not clear | Transparent consent flow, privacy-first messaging |
| GDPR enforcement | Legal counsel, DPA with every institutional customer |
| Slow B2B sales cycle | Start with pilot/free reports to build pipeline faster |
| Low user base in early months | Delay B2B sales until 5,000+ active users |

# Business Proposal: The A* Squad — Community Subscription

**Prepared by:** Battle Plan  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Proposal ref:** BP-006

---

## Executive Summary

The tool is free. The community is the product. A* Squad is a paid cohort-based subscription where students targeting top grades join a group of 40–50 peers studying the same subjects, held accountable through weekly group sessions, shared resources, and mutual pressure to perform. The community does not replace the app — it makes the app sticky, social, and worth paying for. Subscription price: £7.99/month. Marginal cost per additional member: near zero. This is a community-led growth model that turns Battle Plan's most ambitious users into each other's biggest asset.

---

## The Problem

### Isolation is the enemy of A* performance

High-achieving students are often isolated in their revision. They sit in their rooms, work through papers alone, and have no reliable way to know if they are on track relative to their peers. The comparison that drives improvement is missing. Meanwhile, students at elite schools benefit from a culture of academic competitiveness, shared notes, and group study sessions. This advantage is structural and invisible.

### Generic communities don't work

Reddit, Discord servers for A-level students, and Facebook groups are too large and too unfocused to generate accountability. A 10,000-person Discord server does not make you feel accountable to anyone. A group of 48 students — all doing AQA Maths, all targeting A* — with your name on a leaderboard and a live session every Thursday evening, does.

---

## The Opportunity

- A-level students spend an average of £800–£2,400 on revision resources
- A community subscription at £7.99/month over 9 months = £71.91 per student
- Cohort model scales without proportional cost increase
- Community products have 2–3× higher retention than solo tools
- No competitor offers structured, small-cohort accountability communities for A-level students

## How It Works

### Cohort formation

Students apply to join a Squad. Cohorts are formed by:

- **Subject set** — students pick their 3 A-levels; matched with others sharing at least 2
- **Target grade** — A* Squad only admits students targeting A/A* (self-reported, validated by past paper history in Battle Plan)
- **Exam board** — matched within the same board where possible

Cohort size: 40–50 students. Once full, a new cohort opens. Each cohort runs for one academic term (10 weeks).

### What members get

**Weekly live session (Thursday 7pm, 90 min via Google Meet):**
- 20 min: Past paper question walkthrough (shared screen, one student leads)
- 30 min: "Battle round" — everyone does the same timed question, compares answers
- 20 min: Topic focus (voted on by the cohort each week)
- 20 min: Open Q&A + planning for next week

Hosted by a moderator (A-level graduate or university student) for the first 4 weeks, then student-led.

**Private cohort space (Discord server, Battle Plan-managed):**
- #mark-schemes — share mark scheme annotations
- #this-week-papers — what paper did you do this week and what score?
- #topic-help — ask cohort members for explanations
- #leaderboard — auto-updated from Battle Plan scores (opt-in)

**Battle Plan app integration (exclusive features):**
- Squad leaderboard (anonymised percentile: "You're in the top 15% of your cohort this week")
- Weekly cohort average vs personal average
- Shared paper log: see which papers your cohort has done without duplicating
- "Challenge a squadmate" — send a timed question to a peer

**Shared resource vault:**
- Members upload their own mark scheme annotations, flashcard decks, topic summaries
- Rated and filtered by the community
- Subject-specific, board-specific

### Moderator model

Moderators are recruited from top university students (A*/A students in relevant subjects) on a part-time basis:

- 4 live sessions per cohort (first month) + on-call Discord for 10 weeks
- Pay: £150/cohort (or Battle Plan credits if they prefer)
- Sourced from: Oxbridge student societies, UCL/Bristol/Warwick STEM groups

At scale, most sessions are student-led — moderators are for cold-start only.

## Go-to-Market

**Phase 1 — Founding cohort (free):**  
Launch 2 founding cohorts at £0, recruit 96 students who are already Battle Plan users. Capture testimonials and leaderboard data. Use results to prove the model.

**Phase 2 — Paid launch:**  
Open paid enrolment at £7.99/month. Cap at 5 cohorts initially. Use waitlist to create urgency ("42 people waiting for the next Maths A* Squad").

**Phase 3 — Organic growth:**  
Cohort members who get their target grade become the product's best marketing. Share outcomes: "23 of 48 students in Spring 2026 Cohort 1 achieved A or A*." Run targeted TikTok content: "Day in my life as an A* Squad member."

**Phase 4 — School partnership:**  
Offer school licences that enrol an entire class into their own private squad. Integrates with the white-label school product (BP-004).

## Competitive Advantage

- **Small cohort size.** 48 people makes it feel personal. Everyone knows each other within 2 weeks.
- **Objective accountability.** The leaderboard is based on real past paper scores from the app, not self-reported "I studied 4 hours today."
- **Subject-specific.** Not a generic student community — every member is revising the same syllabus.
- **Moderator model.** University students who recently aced these exams are more credible and relatable than teachers.
- **Network effect.** The more cohorts run, the better the shared resource vault becomes.

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Low engagement (ghost members) | Minimum activity requirement to stay in cohort: log 1 paper/week or attend 1 session |
| Moderator quality variance | Moderator training guide, session template, back-up moderator on call |
| Students cancelling after 1 month | Offer 10-week bundle upfront; make the community too valuable to leave |
| Scaling moderator recruitment | Partner with student societies; offer course credit or LinkedIn endorsements |
| Cohort cohesion breaks down | Weekly session structure enforced by template; Discord moderation guidelines |

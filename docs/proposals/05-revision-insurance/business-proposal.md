# Business Proposal: Revision Insurance — Grade Guarantee Product

**Prepared by:** Battle Plan  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Proposal ref:** BP-005

---

## Executive Summary

Students pay £20 to insure against missing their target A-level grade. If they hit it, Battle Plan keeps the premium. If they miss it, they receive a £100 payout. This is not a gimmick — it is a properly structured financial product underwritten by the platform's own risk pool, with eligibility conditions tied to genuine revision effort tracked in the app. Students who use the app consistently are statistically more likely to achieve their targets, so the actuarial math works. For students, it turns revision into a bet they want to win. For Battle Plan, it generates premium revenue with a manageable claims rate.

---

## The Problem

A-level students face an enormous stakes event with almost no safety net. Missing a grade by one mark can cost a university place, a conditional offer, or a career path. The anxiety around this is pervasive and well-documented. Students currently have two options: revise harder, or pay for tutoring. There is no product that says: *"We believe in your ability to hit your target. We'll put money on it too."*

---

## The Opportunity

- 750,000 A-level sitters per year in England
- ~15% are at genuine risk of missing their offer (based on UCAS clearing data)
- Average anxiety about missing grades is highest June–August
- No existing financial product insures against grade outcomes
- The data Battle Plan already holds makes it uniquely positioned to price this risk accurately

## How It Works

### Eligibility (effort-gating)

Insurance is only available to students who have met minimum engagement thresholds in the 60 days before their first exam:

- Logged at least 8 past papers across their subjects
- Average score trend: stable or improving
- No gap longer than 10 days without a logged paper

These conditions serve two purposes:
1. They select for students who are genuinely revising (lower claims risk)
2. They create an incentive to use the app consistently to become eligible

### The policy

| | |
|---|---|
| **Premium** | £20 one-time payment |
| **Cover period** | From purchase to results day (August) |
| **Trigger event** | Student achieves below their stated target grade in any one of their covered subjects |
| **Payout** | £100 per subject claim, maximum 2 subjects per student |
| **Evidence required** | Photo upload of results slip on results day |
| **Verification** | Manual review within 48 hours; £100 paid to student bank account or PayPal |

### Pricing rationale

- Premium: £20
- Maximum payout: £200 (2 subjects × £100)
- Expected claims rate (effort-gated): 20–25%
- Expected payout per policy: £200 × 0.22 = £44
- At £20 premium, this is loss-making per policy — but:
  - Volume effects: at 5,000 policies, £100,000 in premiums, £110,000 in expected claims = -£10,000
  - This is a **loss-leader product** in Year 1 — the value is user acquisition, retention, and press coverage
  - In Year 2–3, with better actuarial data, tighten eligibility to bring claims rate to 15% → profitable at £20 premium

### Path to profitability

| Year | Policies | Premium revenue | Claims rate | Payout | Net |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2,000 | £40,000 | 25% | £50,000 | -£10,000 |
| 2 | 6,000 | £120,000 | 20% | £96,000 | +£24,000 |
| 3 | 15,000 | £300,000 | 17% | £204,000 | +£96,000 |

### The real business model

Revenue insurance is the retention hook, not the profit centre. Students who buy insurance:
- Use the app more consistently (to maintain eligibility)
- Are more likely to renew or recommend (skin in the game)
- Are trackable as a high-intent cohort for upsells (tutoring, premium features)
- Generate press coverage ("revision app puts money on your grades")

The PR value of this product in Year 1 alone is worth the £10,000 Year 1 loss.

## Legal & Regulatory Considerations

This product requires careful legal structuring:

**Option A — Self-insurance (risk pool model)**
Battle Plan operates a ring-fenced risk pool funded by premiums. This avoids FCA insurance regulation but exposes Battle Plan to unlimited liability if claims exceed the pool. Requires a legal cap: "Maximum total claims in any calendar year: £50,000."

**Option B — Regulated insurance product**
Partner with an FCA-authorised insurer who underwrites the risk. Battle Plan acts as an introducer. Insurer handles claims. Battle Plan earns a referral fee per policy (e.g. £5). Lower revenue but zero liability exposure.

**Recommendation: Option B for Year 1.** Partner with a specialist insurer (e.g. a Lloyd's of London syndicate, or a fintech insurer like Qover or Wakam). This transfers all actuarial risk while preserving the marketing benefit.

## Go-to-Market

**Phase 1 (April–May, pre-exam):**  
Introduce eligibility tracker in the app: "You're X papers away from qualifying for Revision Insurance." This drives app engagement before the product is even live.

**Phase 2 (May launch):**  
Open insurance purchase in-app. Promote via student social media (TikTok, Instagram, student Facebook groups). Press release to student media: The Tab, Student Beans, The Guardian Education.

**Phase 3 (August — results day):**  
Process claims quickly and publicly. Share anonymised stats: "87% of insured students hit their target grade." This becomes next year's acquisition campaign.

## Competitive Advantage

- **No one else does this.** Zero competitors in the student insurance space.
- **Proprietary risk data.** Battle Plan has performance trajectory data that no insurer has — this improves pricing accuracy as data accumulates.
- **Viral incentive structure.** Students who tell their friends they've taken out grade insurance create organic word-of-mouth.
- **Engagement flywheel.** Eligibility conditions require consistent app use → more data → better risk models → tighter eligibility → lower claims.

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Claims exceed projections in Year 1 | Cap total annual claims at £50,000; use Option B (insurer partner) to limit exposure |
| Results verification fraud | Manual review; results slips have unique formatting and school headers |
| FCA regulatory risk | Legal advice before launch; Option B (insurer partner) eliminates this risk |
| Negative press if claims denied | Clear, generous policy: if in genuine doubt, pay out |
| Student perception ("the app betting against me") | Framing: "We believe in you. We're putting money on it too." |
